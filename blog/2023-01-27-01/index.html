.mp3’s are Incredible, and Streaming Services Suck

27 January 2023

One of my favorite things I own is my music collection. I’m not here to extoll the virtues of music, but I am here to tell you why Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube Music can all kick rocks.

Service Lock-in

Over the past few years, I’ve jumped between Apple Music and Spotify to try out of their user interfaces. Switching between the two is a huge pain because you have to manually transfer songs or pay for a service to programmatically do it for you with decidedly ok results. If you don’t want to switch because of a social or operating system tie in, be prepared to weather price increases or hardware lockouts.

Streaming isn’t anything special, either. Setting up your own streaming box isn’t more than an hour or two of work with a little bit of research. Why pay someone else in perpetuity to do it for you?

Custom Tags and Art

Because I spend so much time organizing and listening to music I’ve collected, I like to organize and tag it. If Spotify says an album is “Rock” but I think it’s “Classic Rock,” it shouldn’t be that hard to change a string somewhere. Why should a service I pay for restrict my organizational urges?

Sometimes I don’t like the ultra deluxe versions of albums with the 17th alternate take of a song, so I just stash extra files and retag the rest to make the original release. Sergeant Pepper’s album cover looks too saturated? Just download a different copy. Freedom over the medium should be the default.

Proper Ownership

One thing that drove me crazy was Spotify just removing albums I liked inexplicably. I understand the licensing agreements exist, but it really sucks just having music disappear. If I pay someone monthly for something, I think it’s reasonable for it to be there month after month. Unless someone waltzes into my hard disk array and deletes things, it should be there in perpetuity. A win for preserving the arts how I’d like to.

I feel like there’s a lesson here about ownership in the modern age, and why it’s a right that should be vehemently defended against corporate incursions.
