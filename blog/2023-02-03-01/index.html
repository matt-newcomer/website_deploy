.org Domains

3 February 2023

One perennial question people ask me when they first visit this site is why does it have a .org top level domain?

Choosing a TLD is a little more involved than just choosing cool looking words. Someone has to run the DNS servers to route requests to the server that this site is hosted on. Picking a TLD is like choosing a partner to handle some of your backend logistics. .io domains are somewhat infamous for poor uptime and registration services, but they’re super cheap. Internet forums have a horror story pop up every few weeks with bad domain registrars. There’s a balance to be had between registration price and service quality, and .org is good enough for me.

That’s not to say .org has been perfect.

.org was one of the original TLDs registered in 1985. As it pertains to the world wide web, .org is intended as the miscellaneous TLD for organizations that don’t fit anywhere else. That’s me!

Also, because I can. Why have some fancy .cloud or .tech when I can own a certified dad domain?

I wonder how long I can hold onto this domain… I doubt there are VC firms eyeing it.
