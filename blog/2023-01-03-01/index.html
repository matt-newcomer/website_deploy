Eating Only Potatoes for a Week

3 January 2023

Yeah, I was that guy. I wanted to rock the boat a little to see what it did to me, so I decided to take a week to try the potato diet.

I originally learned about the diet from Slime Mold Time Mold’s community trial. Just the prospect of eating one food exclusively was utterly fascinating; well that and I wanted to see what it would do to me. I’ve read about how food impacts your mental state in the past, but I never really noticed that change in myself. Maybe I’ve always eaten a “western” diet, maybe it’s a blind spot I have. Regardless, why not see what happens?

The ground rules are super simple:

  1. Eat potatoes
  2. Eat as many potatoes as you want, but stop when you’re full
  3. Try not to season them, but if you do, avoid dairy and keep the salt down

The Experience

The feeling of going to the store solely to buy potatoes is weird. To run my little experiment I got a 10lb bag of Russet, a 5lb bag of yellow, and a 1lb bag of mini red potatoes. I opted to bake the Russets and roast the yellows and mini reds because that’s how I’ve always prepared each type and just went with it.

Because you are only making potatoes, it’s easy to prep them in bulk. I just fired up the oven once and made roughly four days of food in roughly an hour. I did have to use every bowl I own to store all of the food I made… whoops.

For each of the seven days, I typically ate a late morning and early evening meal. The most I ate was on the last day when I had four Russets in one sitting for dinner. If you decide to do this yourself, you’ll quickly notice just how hard it is to eat potatoes quickly. Even when I tried, it took me five minutes or more to finish a single potato. The speed and the volume that the potatoes take up in your stomach seems to have made a situation where I couldn’t overeat. I guess the second rule above is self-enforcing.

As for the food, it’s like eating solid air. Potatoes are dense and utterly tasteless without garnishes or seasoning. After the first day, I slowly stopped caring. Instead of living to eat, my mindset shifted to one of eating to live. Potatoes are pretty much all carbs, and I experiences some weird after-meal energy spikes and crashes. This was the single hardest thing to overcome with respect to the diet. They became more pronounced as the week went on, but once I resumed a normal diet they went away. I suspect that a lack of protein was the root cause.

The second hardest thing about the diet was the social aspect. When my friends invited me out to lunch or some event had food, I just had to wait for potato time. I did slowly perfect the art of gently saying no without launching into a full story (which you’re reading now).

Takeaways

Dear reader, I’d like to impress upon you how uneventful adhering to this diet is. You buy the potatoes, wash the potatoes, potentially cut the potatoes, you bake/roast/boil the potatoes, you enjoy the potatoes. Repeat weekly.

I genuinely had a good time just eating spuds. So much so that I want to do it again, but with two changes so it’s maintainable in the long term:

  • I can eat a small piece of meat/can of beans every few days
    • Fixes the hardest issue
  • I can skip one meal per week to eat with friends
    • Fixes the second hardest issue

I think my little experiment was a success; so much so that I want to do it for a few months. Let’s see how that goes.

Thank you, humble spud.
